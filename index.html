Why is this important?

Non-standard characters in text can be a sign of potential phishing attempts or other malicious activities. Here's why you should be careful:

  • Phishing: Attackers may use similar-looking characters to create fake URLs or email addresses that appear legitimate at first glance.
  • Obfuscation: Non-standard characters can be used to hide malicious content from security filters.
  • Impersonation: These techniques can be used to create usernames or display names that look like those of trusted entities.

Safety Tips:

  • If you see non-standard characters in a link, do not click it.
  • Be cautious of email addresses using unexpected characters.
  • When in doubt, manually type known, trusted URLs instead of clicking links.
  • Use official apps or verified websites for sensitive operations like banking.
